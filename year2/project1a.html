Project 1a

For the first half of project 1a we worked together as a group to carry out site analysis of the West Hoe area of Plymouth, alongside researching a precedent which for our group was the Barbican Estate in London. Following on from group work, we each designed a dwelling for an individual. The idea for my design was to break the boundary between the residential and park area of West Hoe by making my dwelling a gateway. (Images not to scale).

 

Example work from project 1a group submission. On the left is a chunk of a 500m section of West Hoe, Plymouth. The section of the house shows the atmosphere of the home, there is also a closer detail section of the structural layers. To the right is a sample of the research we conducted of the Barbican Estate, London.

From research about the Barbican Estate, as a group we made a diagramatic model of the estate. The red thread shows the boundary of the site and how movement flows around the perimeter. The black thread shows the movement within the site between the public parks.

Diagrams showing my response to the precedent study and site analysis.

This CAD drawing is of a section through the centre of my site. The individual dwellings are raised of the ground to act as a gateway between the residential area and the west hoe park.

I also created CAD drawings of the plans of my individual dwelling. The first floor plan shows the stairs that lead up to the house from ground level, the kitchen and living area. The second floor plan shows the master bedroom and a bathroom.

The structure of my building was a steel space frame holding up 4 shipping containers. This project was the first time designing with a space frame, to understand the technology behind them I handmade a 1:50 model of my design.
