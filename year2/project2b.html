Project 2b

Project 2b followed on from the analysis of Plymouth and Turin. As a group we recognized that asylum seekers in Europe are not being accepted into the UK enough. The UK aims to accommodate for 20,000 refugees by 2020, our plan would increase that by nearly double. We would bring migrants from Calais by ferry to Plymouth to live in our ‘Sanctuary City’, this would supply the asylum seekers with everything they would need to integrate into society. Plymouth does not have many places to worship for religions other than Christianity so as a response I chose to design a multi-faith centre for the migrants who will be coming from a more diverse range of religions. (Images not to scale)

 

I made individual models for 6 key religions to help me understand what was required for each religion to pray or worship.

Series of models representing materials. The concrete model represents the fabric of the existing building I was working with. The two cardboard models were fabricated on the laser cutter showing the railing system for partitioning the worship space and the diagrid structure forming the free form shell of my design.

Following this I made a 1:50 model of the entrance atrium of my design. The left images show the laser cut diagrid structure. The right images show the laser cut cladding tiles and the handmade pyramids representing the glass pyramids that cover the main gathering space roof.

I also made a section of the multifaith centre focusing on the main gathering space, I used CAD and overlaid Photoshop to add the detail and atmosphere.
