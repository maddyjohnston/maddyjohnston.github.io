Fabricate

Site analysis of the West end of Plymouth and analysis from our study trip to Turin, Italy begin our group development of a masterplan that rejuvenates the West area of Plymouth city centre. Our group recognized the need to give back to people who need help, beginning with our design of market stalls in Turin that help the homeless, which developed into our ‘Sanctuary City’ masterplan that helps integrate migrants into the community. As a response to the group masterplan I designed a multi-faith centre, to offer a place of prayer and worship to the migrants and the wider community.

 

From a visit to Porta Palazzo market in Turin, this is a hand drawn quadrat looking at how the vendors interact with the ground.

The group design of a market stall that has multiple use whether it is daytime or night-time. The canopies can be raised and lowered to accommodate the vendors during the day and offer a place of rest for the homeless during the night. As part of the concept development stage, I created miniature models of each faiths requirements to pray or worship. Having a strong understanding of this was key to this projects success.
